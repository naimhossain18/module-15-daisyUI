Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

A helmet is considered old and needs replacing if it's more than five years old, shows visible signs of wear and tear like cracks, scratches, or damaged padding, has been involved in a crash, or if the interior is significantly worn or coming apart, regardless of its appearance

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that has been in storage, it's not recommended to ride it immediately without first performing thorough checks as the tires may be flat, brakes could be compromised due to sitting, the chain might need lubrication, and the battery may be dead, so it's best to inspect and potentially service it before taking it out on a long ride

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, always wear a helmet, ride with traffic flow, use hand signals when turning, yield to pedestrians, stay aware of your surroundings, ride in a predictable manner, use designated bike lanes when available, and follow all traffic laws applicable to cyclists in your area;
